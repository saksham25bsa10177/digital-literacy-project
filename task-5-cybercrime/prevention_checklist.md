Prevention Checklist: Stay Safe Online

1.Use strong, unique passwords Create different passwords for college portals, email, and social media. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

2.Enable two-factor authentication Turn on OTP/app-based verification for Gmail, banking apps, and social media accounts.

3.Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive work Do not access banking, UPI apps, or college portals on open Wi-Fi networks in cafes or hostels.

4.Check links before clicking Do not click suspicious scholarship, internship, or job links received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email.

5.Keep apps and devices updated Install updates regularly to fix security vulnerabilities and prevent malware.

6.Never share OTP or verification codes Banks, UPI apps, and government agencies never ask for OTP over calls or messages.

7.UPI Safety Tip – Verify before paying Always confirm the receiver’s name before sending money via UPI. Fraudsters may use similar names.

8.UPI Safety Tip – Don’t approve “Collect Requests” blindly Reject unknown payment requests. Scammers often send “request” links claiming prize or refund.

9.Limit personal information on social media Avoid posting phone number, address, ID cards, or college documents publicly.

10.Install apps only from official stores Download from Google Play Store or Apple App Store; avoid APK files from unknown websites.
